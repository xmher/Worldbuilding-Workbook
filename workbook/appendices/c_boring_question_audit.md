# Appendix C. The Boring Question Audit

Every world fails in the small places first. Your magic system can be glorious and your court politics can be intricate, but if your reader notices no one ever bathes, eats, sleeps, or pays for anything, the world thins.

This appendix is a checklist of the un-glamorous questions. None of these need to appear on the page. All of them should have an answer, so when one surfaces you have something better than a shrug.

Use it as a scan. Run down the list. Mark the ones your book already answers. Mark the ones that make you pause. The pauses are where to work.

---

## Money And Trade

- What's the currency? One or many? Coins, notes, barter, trade chits?
- Who mints it? The crown? The temples? A merchant league?
- What's a day's wage? A loaf of bread? A horse? A sword?
- Do the poor and rich use the same money, or different tiers?
- What do people steal? What can't be stolen easily?
- Is there banking? Lending? Interest?
- Is there debt? How's it enforced?

## Food And Drink

- What's the daily meal? The festival meal?
- What's the grain? The meat? The fruit?
- What's the peasant dish? What's the noble dish?
- What's the drink? Is there tea, coffee, chocolate equivalent? Wine? Beer? Spirits?
- What's the spice? Is it local or traded?
- How's food preserved through winter? Salting, smoking, drying, cellars, ice?
- Who cooks? In a great house, in a cottage, on the road?

## Bathing And Hygiene

- Do people bathe? How often? Hot water, cold, river, bath house?
- What do they wash with? Soap? Ash? Herbs?
- What do teeth get cleaned with?
- How do hair and skin get treated?
- Perfume or scent? Who wears it?
- What do people smell like, honestly?

## Menstruation

- How's it handled? Rags, pads, moss, cloth belts, magical solutions?
- Is it private, shameful, celebrated, ritualised?
- Are there taboos? Spaces she can't enter, things she can't do?
- Does magic interact with it?
- Does your heroine have one on the page? If never, why?

## Contraception

- Does it exist? Herbs, magic, ritual, barrier, divine intervention?
- Is it reliable?
- Who controls access? Women, healers, temples, no one?
- Is it stigmatised or normal?
- Does your open-door scene address it? If not, why not?

## Pregnancy And Birth

- Who attends a birth? Midwives, priestesses, healers, family?
- What's the danger level? High mortality, low, magical protection?
- What happens to a child without parents? Orphanages, fostering, temple wards?
- How's paternity established, culturally and legally?
- Are there magical complications? Half-breed pregnancies, bond-children?

## Sleep And Shelter

- Where does the average person sleep? A bed? A pallet? Shared?
- What do they sleep on? Straw, feather, wool?
- What's the night like? Dark, patrolled, dangerous, ordinary?
- How many people per room in a commoner home? In a palace?
- What does a traveller do for shelter? Inns, hospitality, sleeping rough?

## Heating And Cooling

- How do buildings stay warm? Fires, furnaces, magic, body heat, layered clothes?
- How do they stay cool in summer? Shade, water, stone, cellars, magic?
- Who can afford what? Is heating a class marker?
- What does a bedroom feel like in winter?

## Lighting

- What's the light source at night? Candles, oil, torches, magic?
- Who can afford it?
- How late does a commoner stay up? A noble? A student?
- What happens in the dark?

## Clothing And Laundry

- What do people wear, on a normal day? At court? In the field?
- What fabric? Wool, linen, silk, leather, hide, magical cloth?
- Who makes it? Who mends it?
- How's it washed? How often?
- How long does an outfit last? A year? Five years?

## Waste And Water

- Where does the waste go? Chamber pots, latrines, rivers, magic?
- How's water sourced? Wells, rivers, aqueducts, magic?
- Is the water safe?
- What happens in a siege?

## Roads And Travel

- What's the road network like? Maintained, rutted, patrolled, lawless?
- How fast's a horse? A cart? A forced march?
- Who pays for roads? Crown, local lords, tolls?
- Can a woman travel alone safely?
- What about mountain passes, rivers, borders?

## Messages And Mail

- How does news travel? Messenger, courier, bird, magic, pigeon?
- How long for a letter between two named places?
- Can a commoner send a letter?
- Are messages secure? Who reads them?
- How's urgent news spread? Beacons, criers, bells?

## Literacy And Writing

- Who can read? What percentage of your world?
- Who can write? Is it a separate skill?
- What do they write on? Parchment, paper, bark, clay, magic?
- What do they write with?
- Where are books kept? Who owns them?
- Is literacy a class marker, a magical marker, a gender marker?

## Calendar And Time

- How many days in a year? Months, weeks, days?
- What are the months called? The days?
- When's the new year?
- What are the festivals? Who celebrates which?
- How's time told within a day? Bells, sun, water, magic?
- Do different cultures use different calendars?

## Languages

- How many languages in your world?
- Does everyone speak the common tongue? Why?
- Are there sacred languages? Secret ones? Dead ones?
- How's translation handled? A character who speaks many, a spell, a convention?
- Do fae and mortals speak the same language? Dragons? Vampires?

## Healthcare

- Who heals? Priestesses, witches, physicians, magic users?
- What's curable? What's fatal?
- How's pain managed? Herbs, magic, drink, endurance?
- What's the healing tradition of your world?
- Do you know how a specific character treats a fever?

## Death And Grief

- What happens to a body? Burned, buried, returned to the earth, preserved, eaten ritually?
- Who handles the dead? Family, priests, specialist death-workers?
- How's grief shown publicly? Colours, veils, songs, silence?
- How long's the mourning period?
- What happens to property? To titles? To debts?

## Law And Justice

- Who enforces law? Guards, militias, magical means, trial by combat?
- What are the major crimes? The minor ones?
- What are the punishments? Fines, flogging, exile, execution, magic?
- Can a noble be tried the same way as a commoner?
- Is there a court system? Appeal? Mercy?

## Marriage And Family

- How's marriage arranged? Love match, arranged, contractual, magical bond?
- What does a wedding look like?
- Can a marriage be dissolved? How?
- What's legitimacy? What's a bastard's status?
- Can same-sex couples marry? Cross-species? Cross-class?

## Childhood And Education

- When does childhood end? Ten? Sixteen? Never, for the nobility?
- Who raises the children? Parents, nursemaids, fosterage?
- Who teaches? Family, tutors, temples, academies?
- When does a child become a legal adult?
- Do children work? What at, what age?

## Class And Rank

- What are the tiers? Royal, noble, clerical, merchant, craftsman, labourer, slave?
- Can you move up? Down? How?
- How's rank visible? Dress, name, speech, bearing?
- What's the rule on crossing class in romance?

## Religion And Worship

- Who do people pray to? Named gods, abstract powers, ancestors, nothing?
- What do they ask for? Harvest, protection, love, vengeance?
- Who leads worship? Priests, priestesses, lay practitioners?
- Where do they worship? Temples, homes, groves, anywhere?
- How's religion entangled with politics?

## Magic In Daily Life

- Who uses magic day to day? Healers, farmers, cooks, no one?
- What's magic cost, in a small working?
- What's the peasant relationship to magic? Suspicious, dependent, indifferent?
- Are there magical conveniences (lighting, cleaning, warming, cooling)?
- Does the mundane world coexist with magic, or is magic rare?

## Animals And Livestock

- What beasts are domestic? Horses, dogs, cats, dragons (in some worlds)?
- What are people raising? Cattle, sheep, goats, poultry, magical beasts?
- What do animals cost, socially and monetarily?
- Is there magical animal speech? Who can use it?

## Sex And Sexuality

- What's the cultural attitude? Puritan, relaxed, ritualised?
- Who can have sex with whom, openly?
- Is premarital sex condoned? Marital sex required? Extramarital sex tolerated?
- How's sex work handled?
- How do fae / vampires / shifters think about mortal sexuality? Differently?

## Gender

- What's the gender system? Two, three, more?
- What roles are gendered? What aren't?
- How's transgression handled?
- Who fights? Who rules? Who inherits?
- Is your world's gender system the same as the real world's, or different?

## Entertainment

- What do people do for fun? Singing, dancing, games, sport, theatre, gambling?
- What's the tavern scene? The court scene? The village scene?
- What's the popular story-form? Bards, theatre, printed books?
- Do your characters ever laugh, play, or rest?

## Ordinary Work

- What does a commoner do in a day?
- What does a noble do in a day?
- What does your heroine do when she's not at the centre of the plot?
- What does your love interest do when he's not pining?
- What's the tedious part of each of their lives?

---

## How To Use This Appendix

- **Scan.** Read the whole list. Mark every question you can't answer about your world.
- **Prioritise.** Pick the five most likely to surface in your book.
- **Answer each one.** One or two sentences. You're not writing an encyclopedia; you're answering for yourself.
- **Re-scan every draft.** New questions surface as the book evolves.

The boring questions don't go in your book. The confidence from having answered them does.
