## The PlotBrew Philosophy

In case you skipped the front matter: here's what I believe, condensed.

- **Romance is the primary load.** Worldbuilding serves the pairing, not the other way.
- **The world's a pressure system.** Every rule, every law, every magic cost: it's there to push your couple together or apart.
- **Craft's specific.** Vague worldbuilding produces vague books. The specific detail's the one readers remember.
- **You can do this.** Writing a romantasy's hard. It's also learnable. The workbook's proof: no magic, just questions and iteration.

That's it. The rest is you, drafting.

## One Last Thing

Writing a romantasy's hard. Not because it should be easy and you're failing; because it's genuinely hard. You're holding a world, a pairing, a plot, a voice, a heat level, a tone, a genre contract, and a personal vision, all at once, for three hundred to five hundred pages.

Be patient with yourself. Be kind. The book gets made the way all books get made: one bad sentence at a time, until the bad sentences become passable and the passable ones become good.

You've got this.
