# Part 6. The Romantasy Layer

Welcome to the heart of the workbook.

Everything before this part has been groundwork. The map, the species, the courts, the magic, the culture. All of it matters, but none of it is why a reader picks up a romantasy at nine on a Tuesday night. They pick it up for the couple. The world is what pressures the couple into existing.

Part 6 is where that pressure gets built.

## Six Clusters

Eighteen sections, grouped into six clusters.

- **Bond foundations.** The taxonomy of mate bonds and the blueprint for your own.
- **Courtship and ceremony.** How your world does the dance, the vows, the claiming.
- **Biology and cycles.** Heat, rut, seasons, pregnancy, lifespan, compatibility.
- **Magical intimacy.** True names, bargains, dreams, and the sex-magic question.
- **Power and structure.** Who holds rank, who holds multiple partners, who gets named.
- **The thesis spine.** Forbidden love as engine, and consent as the whole game.

Each section is short. Prompts come at three tiers. The last prompt is always the same question asked a different way: how does this piece of worldbuilding put pressure on your couple?

## Working Through It

Read the cluster intros. Skim for what's relevant to your book.

If you're writing a monster romance with no dragons, skim the dragon sections but don't draft them. For a slow-burn enemies-to-lovers political fae book, the polyamory section might not be yours today, and that's fine. Skip the omegaverse pages if A/B/O isn't your genre; come back to them if you're curious.

The one section you shouldn't skip is 48 (Consent Scaffolding), whatever heat level you're writing. Even sweet books make consent choices. Making them deliberately is the job.

## A Quick Word On Range

This part covers the full heat range of the genre: closed-door romance, fade-to-black, open-door with consent beats, and the darker end with captivity, dubcon, breeding kinks, and possession tropes. None of it is judged. All of it is designed.

Sweet books still use most of this part. Courtship rituals and bond mechanics aren't heat-gated. Dark books need every prompt in sections 42 and 48.

Write what you love. Then build the world that earns it.
